Safety & Stability

Safest countries for expats.

Safety statistics are a starting point, not the full picture. The Global Peace Index measures societal safety, conflict, and militarization across 163 countries — it’s the most consistent neutral benchmark available. But the index doesn’t capture what daily life actually feels like, or whether infrastructure and institutions are stable enough to depend on long-term. These six destinations score well on both counts — the numbers and the lived experience.

The U.S. ranks 128th out of 163 on the 2025 Global Peace Index. Most popular retirement destinations score dramatically better. That context matters when evaluating risk.

Portugal

GPI #7 globally · Violent crime rare · Stable institutions

GPI #7 globally Low violent crime EU institutional stability D7 visa path $$

Portugal ranks 7th in the world on the 2025 Global Peace Index — one of the safest countries on the planet by any measure. Expats consistently report a practical experience that matches the statistics: walking alone at night in Lisbon, Porto, or the Algarve is unremarkable. Violent crime is genuinely rare. The country has stable democratic institutions, no significant civil unrest, and the long-term security of EU membership. For someone choosing where to put down roots, Portugal combines the highest safety metrics with an accessible residency path.

verify current D7 income threshold

Best for

Anyone for whom long-term institutional stability, the lowest crime environment, and EU residency matter most.

Think twice if

Budget is very tight — coastal Portugal has become expensive. Interior towns are where the D7 math works on a modest income.

02 of 06

Uruguay

Most stable democracy in South America · Low corruption · GPI top tier

Most stable S. American democracy Low corruption 10-yr foreign income tax exemption Montevideo walkable $$

Uruguay is consistently ranked the most politically stable and least corrupt country in South America — not by a small margin. It has strong rule of law, transparent institutions, and a long democratic tradition. Violent crime is low by regional standards; Montevideo’s expat neighborhoods like Pocitos and Punta Carretas are genuinely safe for daily life. Uruguay also offers a 10-year exemption on foreign income taxes for new residents — Social Security, pensions, and investment income are not taxed locally for the first decade, with an option for a permanent 7% flat rate afterward.

verify current residency income requirements & tax exemption rules with a qualified advisor

Best for

People who want South American affordability with European-style institutional stability, and a significant tax benefit on foreign income.

Think twice if

Proximity to the U.S. is a priority — Uruguay is a long flight from most American cities, with no quick route home.

03 of 06

Costa Rica

Safest in Central America · No standing army · Stable democracy

Safest Central American country Abolished military 1948 Stable democracy Strong expat community $–$$

Costa Rica abolished its military in 1948 and has maintained one of the most stable democracies in Latin America ever since. It consistently ranks as the safest country in Central America and among the most peaceful in the Western Hemisphere. The pura vida culture is genuinely welcoming, and the large expat communities in the Central Valley provide the “there’s always someone to call” safety net that matters particularly for people moving alone. The combination of safety, proximity to the U.S., and accessible residency makes it a first-look destination for many people.

verify current Pensionado threshold

Best for

People who want the safest option in Latin America with a ready-made expat community and a short flight home.

Think twice if

You want European-level institutional stability — Costa Rica is the safest in Central America, but that’s a different benchmark than Portugal or Uruguay.

04 of 06

Spain

GPI #25 globally · Dense safe cities · Strong legal protections

GPI #25 globally Low violent crime Active street life Strong legal framework $$–$$$

Spain ranks 25th on the 2025 Global Peace Index — dramatically safer than the U.S. by any measure. Dense, well-lit cities with active street life mean that walking alone, dining solo, and going about daily business without a second thought is normal rather than notable. Violent crime is rare; petty theft in tourist areas requires the usual awareness but is a different category from violence. Valencia is the practical standout: flat, warm, walkable, and genuinely safer feeling than many European capitals.

verify current NLV income requirements

Best for

People who want a safe, active European city where daily independence feels completely natural.

Think twice if

The NLV income threshold is a stretch — Portugal’s D7 is more accessible and comparable in safety.

05 of 06

Panama

Stable economy · USD · Well-established expat infrastructure

dollarized economy Pensionado visa Political stability El Cangrejo safe expat hub $$

Panama has a long track record of political and economic stability relative to its Central American neighbors — the dollarized economy, Canal-driven revenue, and established banking sector contribute to an institutional steadiness that shows in daily life. El Cangrejo in Panama City is a well-established expat neighborhood with active streets, building security, and a mature support infrastructure. The Pensionado program has been in place for decades and the country has strong incentives to remain welcoming to foreign retirees.

verify current Pensionado requirements & neighborhood-level safety for your target area

Best for

People who want Latin American affordability with above-average institutional stability, a USD economy, and a well-worn expat infrastructure.

Think twice if

Climate is a concern — Panama City is tropical. Boquete gives you the same institutional security in a far more comfortable highland climate.

06 of 06

New Zealand

GPI top 5 globally · English-speaking · Excellent healthcare · Visa complex

GPI top 5 globally English official language Excellent public healthcare Visa path complex for Americans $$–$$$

New Zealand consistently ranks in the top 5 safest countries in the world on the Global Peace Index. It’s English-speaking, has excellent public healthcare, strong institutions, and genuine quality of life in cities like Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch. For someone whose top priority is safety and stability above all else, it belongs on the list. The climate in the north is mild and subtropical; the South Island is cooler and more dramatic.

The honest caveat is significant: New Zealand does not have a straightforward retirement visa for Americans. Residency paths typically require a job offer, significant investment, or a family connection. This is a destination worth researching carefully if it appeals, but don’t assume the path is simple — it isn’t.

verify current New Zealand residency options for U.S. citizens specifically — paths are limited

Best for

People for whom maximum safety and English-speaking stability outweigh cost and visa complexity — and who have the resources and patience to navigate the residency path.

Think twice if

A straightforward residency visa is important — New Zealand’s paths for Americans are genuinely limited and worth researching before getting attached to the idea.
