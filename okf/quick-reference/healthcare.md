# Healthcare & Insurance

**Can you use Medicare overseas?** No — Medicare does not cover you outside the US with extremely limited exceptions. You need to replace it entirely with public healthcare (if you qualify as a resident), private international insurance, or both.

**Does expat health insurance cover pre-existing conditions?** Varies significantly. Some exclude pre-existing conditions entirely, others cover with waiting periods, a few cover from day one at higher premiums. Public systems in Costa Rica, Portugal, and France cover residents with no exclusions.

**What happens if your health declines while living abroad?** Depends on where you are and what coverage you have. Countries with strong public systems (France, Portugal, Costa Rica) provide meaningful safety nets. Others require private insurance or out-of-pocket payment.

**Best countries for English-speaking healthcare?** Malta and Belize have English as an official language. Malaysia's private hospitals operate largely in English. Panama City, Medellín, and major Costa Rican hospitals have English-speaking staff.

**Cost of expat health insurance for seniors?** Rough range $150–400/month for people 55–65 for international private insurance. Costa Rica's Caja ~$60–100/month for residents. France covers serious chronic conditions at 100% once enrolled.
