# Mobility & Accessibility

**Most flat and walkable cities to retire abroad?** Valencia Spain (200km pedestrian infrastructure), Medellín's El Poblado and Laureles, Cuenca Ecuador historic center, Mérida Mexico, Montevideo. Avoid: Lisbon, Porto, Dubrovnik, San Miguel de Allende — hilly with cobblestones.

**What to look for in accessible housing?** Single-level or elevator access, step-free entry, wider doorways, ground or low floor, proximity to essentials. Newer construction is more accessible than historic buildings.

**Best public transit for seniors with limited mobility?** Medellín's metro and cable car system. Valencia's tram and metro. Montevideo's urban transit. Research whether your specific neighborhood has transit access.

**Expat cities with cobblestone streets to avoid?** Lisbon, Porto, Dubrovnik, San Miguel de Allende, Rome and Florence historic centers, Valletta Malta. Newer neighborhoods within these cities are often paved.

**Best countries to retire with a progressive illness?** France (100% coverage for serious conditions, no exclusions). Portugal and Spain (good specialist access, no pre-existing exclusions in public systems). Costa Rica's Caja. Colombia (world-class private hospitals at low cost).
