# Money & Income

**Can I collect SSDI while living abroad?** For most recipients yes — SSDI follows you overseas as long as you remain a US citizen or meet certain residency conditions. About 25 countries have restrictions. Verify your specific benefit type and destination.

**Does Social Security pay if you live outside the US?** Most retirement and disability benefits continue. SSI stops when you leave the US. Verify your specific benefit type.

**Which countries use the US dollar?** Panama and Ecuador are the most popular retirement destinations using the dollar. El Salvador also uses it. No exchange rate risk, no currency math.

**Minimum income for a retirement visa?** Panama ~$1,000/month. Costa Rica ~$1,000/month. Ecuador ~$1,446/month. Portugal D7 ~€920/month. Spain NLV ~€2,400/month. Always verify current figures with the consulate.

**Is US Social Security taxed if you live abroad?** The US may still tax Social Security depending on combined income. Some countries have tax treaties. A tax professional familiar with expat situations is essential.

**Hidden costs of retiring abroad?** The move itself, international health insurance before residency, return flights home, currency conversion fees, gap between tourist and resident prices, residency legal fees.
