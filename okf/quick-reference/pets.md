# Pets & Relocation

**Pet import rules by country?** Most countries require: microchip, up-to-date rabies vaccination, health certificate from USDA-accredited vet, official USDA endorsement. Some require rabies titer tests taking months. EU countries follow a standardized framework. Thailand requires 30-day quarantine. Australia and New Zealand have strict quarantine.

**Most pet-friendly countries to retire?** Costa Rica and Panama — straightforward entry, no quarantine. Most of continental Europe follows EU pet passport framework. Ecuador is relatively easy. Thailand requires quarantine.

**Is it safe to fly an older pet in cargo?** Most vets advise against cargo for senior pets, brachycephalic breeds, or pets with health conditions. Cabin travel is safer but limited to small pets. Get veterinary input before deciding.

**Which countries require quarantine?** Australia, New Zealand, Hawaii, Thailand (30 days). Most of continental Europe, Latin America, and Southeast Asia do not require quarantine if paperwork is in order.

**How early to start the pet relocation process?** At least 6 months out, longer if destination requires a rabies titer test. Leaving this until the last few months is one of the most common and stressful mistakes.
