# Planning & Logistics

**Where to start when researching retirement abroad?** Start with yourself, not countries. Get clear on exact monthly income, healthcare requirements, mobility needs, pets, and how far you're willing to be from the US. Most people do this backward.

**Is it worth shipping your car overseas?** Usually not. Shipping costs $1,000–$5,000+, many countries have age restrictions, import taxes add cost. Most expat destinations have affordable local transportation.

**What documents do I need to move abroad?** Valid passport (renew if within 6 months of expiring), birth certificate with apostille, proof of income, background check with apostille, marriage or divorce certificates if relevant. Start 12+ months out.

**How long does the residency process take?** Plan for 6–18 months from starting document collection to approved residency. Panama and Costa Rica are among the faster options. European visas typically take longer.

**What to do before leaving the US?** Renew passport, gather and apostille documents, research healthcare coverage, set up US mail forwarding, notify Social Security of move if required, open fee-free international bank account, arrange first-week local currency.

**What to set up in the first 90 days after arrival?** Register with local authorities, activate local healthcare coverage, open local bank account, register pets locally if needed, find essential services, connect with expat community.
